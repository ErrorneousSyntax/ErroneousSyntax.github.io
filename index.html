Dimensionality Reduction in Clustering

In clustering and other unsupervised learning tasks, dimensionality reduction techniques like t-SNE and UMAP play a crucial role. High-dimensional datasets often make it difficult to visualize patterns or identify meaningful clusters because the data points are sparsely distributed across many dimensions. Methods like t-SNE and UMAP transform the data into a lower-dimensional space while preserving the local and global structure, making clusters more apparent and easier to analyze. This not only improves visualization but can also enhance the performance of clustering algorithms by focusing on the most relevant relationships in the data.
